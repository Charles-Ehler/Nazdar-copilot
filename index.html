Think back to your last project meeting. Could you name every decision and who owns the follow-ups? If you would have to dig through your notes, this course is for you.

AI at Nazdar works for you. Not the other way around.

Copilot gives you back the most valuable thing you have at work: your time.

Copilot is for
  • Taking notes so you can focus on the conversation
  • Writing meeting recaps after the call ends
  • Drafting follow-up emails based on decisions made
  • Catching you up when you join a meeting late
Copilot is not
  • A replacement for your judgment or expertise
  • A substitute for your seat at the table
  • A tool that skips the need to review its output before you send it
  • Appropriate for confidential HR, legal, or personnel conversations

This course is for employees with a Microsoft 365 Copilot license. Not sure if you have one? Check with IT.

Did you know? People who use Copilot in meetings get their action items written for them automatically. No more scribbling notes while trying to listen.

Before: 30 seconds of setup.

  • Turn on transcription. Copilot needs the transcript to work. No transcript means no recap.
  • Let attendees know the meeting is being transcribed. A quick heads-up at the start is the professional standard.
  • Walk in prepared. Copilot works best alongside someone who already knows the context.

The Top 3 Rules

1
You are the editor.
Review every Copilot output before you act on it or send it. Copilot does the drafting. You do the deciding.
2
Transcription on. Attendees informed.
Every meeting, every time. Turn on transcription before the call starts. Tell attendees the meeting is being transcribed.
3
Confidential stays confidential.
No HR, legal, or personnel discussions with Copilot in the room. When in doubt, leave it out.
